# Action Verbs: Research & Analysis

Strong résumés lead each bullet with a precise action verb. This reference organizes verbs by **use case** so you can quickly find the right word for what you actually did—whether that was running a regression, coding interview transcripts, or debugging a data pipeline.

> **How to use this list:** Find the category that matches your experience, pick a verb, and build a concise bullet that includes *what* you did, *how/for whom*, and *the result or scale*.

---

### Research & Investigation

*Use when describing work that involves finding, collecting, or uncovering information—fieldwork, literature reviews, lab studies, or independent inquiry.*

- **Investigated** — (Investigated patterns in sleep behavior across a 50-participant psychology study)
- **Explored** — (Explored alternative renewable energy materials through a semester-long independent study)
- **Surveyed** — (Surveyed 150 undergraduate students on campus food insecurity using a Qualtilla instrument)
- **Examined** — (Examined archival court records to support a legal anthropology research project)
- **Researched** — (Researched peer-reviewed literature on cognitive behavioral therapy for a capstone paper)
- **Reviewed** — (Reviewed 40+ journal articles to build an annotated bibliography on urban migration)
- **Observed** — (Observed preschool classroom interactions for a developmental psychology practicum)
- **Documented** — (Documented soil sample characteristics across 12 field sites for an ecology lab)
- **Identified** — (Identified three recurring themes in participant narratives during a qualitative study)
- **Discovered** — (Discovered a cataloging error affecting 200+ records while auditing the departmental archive)
- **Mapped** — (Mapped community resource locations using GIS software for a public health capstone)
- **Compiled** — (Compiled a database of 500+ press releases for a media-framing content analysis)
- **Gathered** — (Gathered ethnographic field notes over a six-week participant-observation period)
- **Collected** — (Collected water quality samples from eight local watersheds for environmental testing)
- **Sourced** — (Sourced primary documents from three university archives for a history honors thesis)
- **Traced** — (Traced the genealogy of a philosophical argument across four centuries of published work)
- **Cataloged** — (Cataloged 300+ archaeological artifacts for a museum digitization project)

### Quantitative Methods

*Use when describing statistical, mathematical, or computational work—modeling, testing, or measurement design.*

- **Forecasted** — (Forecasted next-quarter demand for three product lines using time-series analysis)
- **Projected** — (Projected student enrollment figures for the next academic year using linear trend models)
- **Estimated** — (Estimated sample size requirements for a proposed clinical nutrition study)
- **Derived** — (Derived a simplified formula for heat transfer in a sophomore-level thermodynamics project)
- **Simulated** — (Simulated traffic flow patterns in MATLAB to evaluate a proposed intersection redesign)
- **Sampled** — (Sampled 300 registered voters using stratified random selection for a political science survey)
- **Tested** — (Tested three competing hypotheses using independent-samples t-tests in SPSS)
- **Standardized** — (Standardized measurement protocols across four lab stations to reduce variability)
- **Weighted** — (Weighted survey responses by demographic proportions to correct for sampling bias)
- **Stratified** — (Stratified a dataset by income bracket before running subgroup analyses)
- **Calibrated** — (Calibrated laboratory instruments before each experimental session to ensure precision)
- **Averaged** — (Averaged daily temperature readings across 30 sensors for a climate monitoring project)
- **Graphed** — (Graphed the distribution of response times to check for normality before hypothesis testing)

- **Computed** — (Computed confidence intervals for each treatment group in a biostatistics assignment)
