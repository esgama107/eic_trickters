# Action Verbs: Leadership & Operations

Whether you ran a student organization, coordinated a campus event, built a prototype in a maker lab, or designed graphics for a club, you exercised leadership and operational skills worth highlighting. This reference groups verbs by **functional area** so you can match the right verb to the role you played.

> **How to use this list:** Find the category closest to your experience, choose a verb that precisely describes your contribution, and follow it with *what*, *how/for whom*, and *the result or scope*.

---

### Leadership & Management

*Use when you directed people, made decisions, set direction, or took initiative—officer roles, team leads, project ownership, or founding new efforts.*

- **Led** — (Led a team of six volunteers to organize a campus blood drive that collected 85 units)
- **Directed** — (Directed rehearsals for a 20-person student theater production over an eight-week schedule)
- **Supervised** — (Supervised four part-time student workers at the campus recreation center front desk)
- **Managed** — (Managed a $5,000 semester budget for a cultural student organization)
- **Oversaw** — (Oversaw daily operations of a student-run coffee shop serving 200+ customers per week)
- **Headed** — (Headed the marketing committee for a 500-attendee engineering career fair)
- **Delegated** — (Delegated tasks across a 10-person planning committee to meet a two-week event deadline)
- **Spearheaded** — (Spearheaded a campus sustainability audit that resulted in three policy recommendations)
- **Chaired** — (Chaired the student government finance committee and reviewed 15+ budget proposals per semester)
- **Mobilized** — (Mobilized 30 student volunteers for a weekend Habitat for Humanity build)
- **Established** — (Established a peer tutoring program that served 60 students in its first semester)
- **Founded** — (Founded a data science club that grew to 45 members within one academic year)
- **Initiated** — (Initiated a mentorship pairing program connecting upperclassmen with incoming transfer students)
