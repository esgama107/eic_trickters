# Tutoring & Teaching — Resume Bullet Examples

> These examples show how to turn tutoring and teaching experiences into
> strong, results-oriented resume bullets. Each one is broken down so you
> can see the thinking behind the final line.

## Bullet Formulas Used in This File

| Formula | Structure | Best For |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **XYZ** | "Accomplished **X** as measured by **Y**, by doing **Z**" | Roles where you can point to a clear metric |
| **CAR** | **Challenge → Action → Result** | Situations where you solved a visible problem |
| **Narrative** | A concise story arc in one sentence (who, what, so-what) | Experiences that are best understood through context |

---

### 1. Peer Tutoring in Statistics

**Context:** Many first-year students in introductory statistics struggled
with hypothesis testing and were at risk of falling below a passing grade.
The university's tutoring center paired upperclassmen with small groups of
three to five students each semester.

**Action:** Designed a series of eight practice problem sets that walked
tutees through each step of a hypothesis test, held twice-weekly drop-in
sessions, and created a one-page reference guide that students could use
during exams.

**Result:** Tutees' average exam scores rose from 64 % to 79 % over one
semester, and the tutoring center adopted the reference guide for all
future statistics sections.

**Resume bullet:**
*Raised tutees' average exam scores by 15 points (64 % → 79 %) by
designing 8 scaffolded problem sets and a quick-reference guide adopted
center-wide for introductory statistics.*

**Formula used:** XYZ

### 2. Teaching Assistant — Grading & Feedback

**Context:** As a TA for a 120-student Introduction to Sociology course,
turnaround time on essay assignments had historically been two weeks,
which left students without feedback before the next paper was due.

**Action:** Built a structured rubric with four scoring dimensions and
wrote a bank of common-feedback comments, enabling faster yet more
consistent grading. Coordinated with two fellow TAs to calibrate scores
on a shared sample of papers before each grading cycle.

**Result:** Reduced average grading turnaround from 14 days to 5 days
while maintaining grading consistency (inter-rater agreement rose from
72 % to 91 %).

**Resume bullet:**
*Cut essay grading turnaround from 14 to 5 days for a 120-student
sociology course by creating a standardized rubric and calibrating scores
across a 3-person TA team, improving inter-rater agreement to 91 %.*

**Formula used:** CAR

### 3. Workshop Facilitation — Financial Literacy

**Context:** A campus resource center wanted to offer financial-literacy
workshops for first-generation college students but had no existing
curriculum. Attendance at previous center events had averaged fewer than
10 participants.

**Action:** Researched student pain points through a 50-response survey,
then developed and facilitated a four-session workshop series covering
budgeting, student-loan repayment, and building credit. Promoted sessions
through social media flyers and partnerships with three student
organizations.

**Result:** Attracted 35 attendees per session on average — more than
triple prior event attendance — and 88 % of post-workshop survey
respondents reported feeling "confident" or "very confident" managing
their personal budget.

**Resume bullet:**
*Launched a 4-session financial-literacy workshop series for
first-generation students, tripling average event attendance to 35
participants and achieving an 88 % confidence rating in post-session
surveys.*

**Formula used:** Narrative

### 4. After-School Program — Middle-School Mentoring

**Context:** A community nonprofit needed volunteers to staff its
after-school homework-help program at a Title I middle school where
fewer than 40 % of eighth-graders were reading at grade level.

**Action:** Volunteered 6 hours per week for two semesters, leading a
reading group of 8 students. Incorporated high-interest books chosen by
the students and introduced brief vocabulary-building games at the start
of each session.

**Result:** Six of the eight students moved up at least one reading level
on the district's benchmark assessment, and program enrollment grew from
12 to 22 students after word-of-mouth referrals.

**Resume bullet:**
*Mentored 8 middle-school students in a Title I after-school reading
program for two semesters, helping 75 % advance at least one reading
level while contributing to a program enrollment increase from 12 to
22 students.*

**Formula used:** XYZ

### 5. Online Tutoring — ESL Conversation Practice

**Context:** During the shift to remote learning, international students
at the university's language center had fewer opportunities to practice
conversational English outside of class.

**Action:** Organized and led weekly one-hour virtual conversation
circles on Zoom for groups of 6–8 students, using structured discussion
prompts tied to current events. Created a shared Google Doc after each
session summarizing new vocabulary and idioms covered.

**Result:** Maintained a consistent cohort of 7 students across 14 weeks;
participants' speaking-assessment scores improved by an average of 12 %,
and the language center continued the program the following year.

**Resume bullet:**
*Facilitated 14 weekly virtual ESL conversation circles for 7
international students, contributing to a 12 % average improvement in
speaking-assessment scores and establishing an ongoing program at the
university language center.*

**Formula used:** CAR
