# Fieldwork & Research — Resume Bullet Examples

> These examples show how to translate research and fieldwork experiences
> into compelling resume bullets, even when the work was unpublished or
> part of a class project.

## Bullet Formulas Used in This File

| Formula | Structure | Best For |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **XYZ** | "Accomplished **X** as measured by **Y**, by doing **Z**" | Roles where you can point to a clear metric |
| **CAR** | **Challenge → Action → Result** | Situations where you solved a visible problem |
| **Narrative** | A concise story arc in one sentence (who, what, so-what) | Experiences that are best understood through context |

---

### 1. Lab Research Assistant — Environmental Science

**Context:** A faculty member's environmental-science lab was studying
microplastic concentrations in local waterways but was behind schedule on
sample processing due to a backlog of 200+ unanalyzed water samples.

**Action:** Joined the lab as an undergraduate research assistant,
learned the FTIR spectroscopy protocol within two weeks, and processed
an average of 15 samples per week. Maintained a detailed sample-tracking
spreadsheet and flagged anomalous readings for the lead researcher.

**Result:** Cleared the sample backlog in 14 weeks, contributing data to
a manuscript that was submitted to *Environmental Pollution*. Earned a
co-authorship acknowledgment for data-collection contributions.

**Resume bullet:**
*Processed 200+ water samples using FTIR spectroscopy over 14 weeks as
an undergraduate research assistant, clearing a semester-long backlog and
contributing data to a manuscript submitted to Environmental Pollution.*

**Formula used:** XYZ

### 2. Survey Data Collection — Public Health Capstone

**Context:** For a public-health capstone project, the research team
needed to gather primary data on food-access barriers in a low-income
urban neighborhood, but the community had low trust in university
researchers due to past negative experiences.

**Action:** Partnered with two local community organizations to
co-design a culturally sensitive survey instrument in English and Spanish.
Conducted 85 in-person interviews at community centers, grocery stores,
and bus stops over four weekends, using a tablet-based form to ensure
real-time data validation.

**Result:** Achieved an 85-interview completion rate with less than 3 %
missing data, and the findings were presented to the city's food-policy
council, informing a grant application for a new mobile produce market.

**Resume bullet:**
*Collected 85 bilingual community surveys on food access with < 3 %
missing data by partnering with local organizations, producing findings
presented to the city food-policy council and supporting a mobile-market
grant application.*

**Formula used:** CAR

### 3. Ethnographic Fieldwork — Cultural Anthropology

**Context:** As part of a senior cultural-anthropology seminar, students
were required to complete an independent ethnographic project. The chosen
site was a multigenerational community garden in an immigrant
neighborhood.

**Action:** Spent 10 weeks conducting participant observation (40+ hours
total) and completed 12 semi-structured interviews with gardeners ranging
in age from 19 to 74. Coded interview transcripts thematically and
triangulated findings with field notes and photographs.

**Result:** Produced a 30-page ethnographic report identifying three
central themes around cultural identity and food sovereignty. The
professor selected the paper for inclusion in the department's
undergraduate research showcase.

**Resume bullet:**
*Conducted a 10-week ethnographic study including 12 semi-structured
interviews and 40+ hours of participant observation at a community
garden, producing a report selected for the department's undergraduate
research showcase.*

**Formula used:** Narrative

### 4. Thesis Research — Political Science

**Context:** An honors-thesis project examined voter-mobilization
strategies among Latino community organizations in a mid-size city. No
existing dataset covered the specific organizations or election cycles of
interest.

**Action:** Built an original dataset by coding 150 social-media posts,
press releases, and flyers from five organizations across three election
cycles. Performed logistic regression analysis to test whether
door-to-door canvassing correlated with higher precinct-level turnout
compared to digital-only outreach.

**Result:** Found a statistically significant positive relationship
(p < 0.05) between canvassing intensity and turnout. Defended the thesis
with honors and presented findings at the Midwest Political Science
Association undergraduate panel.

**Resume bullet:**
*Built an original dataset of 150 coded communications across 5
organizations and 3 election cycles for an honors thesis on Latino
voter mobilization, finding a significant canvassing–turnout
relationship (p < 0.05) and presenting at MPSA.*

**Formula used:** XYZ

### 5. Literature Review — Psychology Research Team

**Context:** A psychology professor's research team was preparing a grant
proposal on the effects of bilingualism on executive function in children
but needed a comprehensive review of the prior decade's literature to
establish the theoretical foundation.

**Action:** Systematically searched PsycINFO, PubMed, and Google Scholar
using predefined inclusion criteria, screening 320 abstracts and
full-text reviewing 47 articles. Organized findings into a summary matrix
categorized by methodology, age group, and outcome measures.

**Result:** Delivered a 15-page annotated literature review two weeks
ahead of the grant deadline. The review was incorporated into the
proposal's background section, and the team received a $45,000 internal
research grant.

**Resume bullet:**
*Screened 320 abstracts and synthesized 47 articles into a 15-page
annotated literature review on bilingualism and executive function,
delivered two weeks early and incorporated into a successful $45 K grant
proposal.*

**Formula used:** CAR
