# Writing & Editing — Resume Bullet Examples

> These examples demonstrate how to present writing and editing
> experience on a resume, whether the work was academic, journalistic,
> organizational, or freelance.

## Bullet Formulas Used in This File

| Formula | Structure | Best For |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **XYZ** | "Accomplished **X** as measured by **Y**, by doing **Z**" | Roles where you can point to a clear metric |
| **CAR** | **Challenge → Action → Result** | Situations where you solved a visible problem |
| **Narrative** | A concise story arc in one sentence (who, what, so-what) | Experiences that are best understood through context |

---

### 1. Blog Writing — Campus Sustainability Office

**Context:** The university's sustainability office had a website with
outdated content and very little student engagement. Web analytics showed
an average of 90 page views per month, and the office wanted to use
storytelling to increase awareness of campus green initiatives.

**Action:** Pitched and wrote two blog posts per month for an academic
year (18 posts total), covering topics from composting programs to
student-led energy audits. Interviewed stakeholders, took original
photographs, and optimized each post with relevant keywords and internal
links.

**Result:** Monthly page views grew from 90 to 340 over eight months, and
the office's Instagram following increased by 25 % after posts were
cross-promoted on social media.

**Resume bullet:**
*Authored 18 blog posts for the campus sustainability office over one
academic year, growing monthly web traffic from 90 to 340 page views and
increasing the office's Instagram following by 25 %.*

**Formula used:** XYZ

### 2. Editing — Student Newspaper

**Context:** The student newspaper's opinion section regularly received
reader complaints about factual errors and inconsistent style, which
undermined the publication's credibility on campus.

**Action:** Served as opinion-section editor for two semesters, reviewing
and editing 6–8 submissions per weekly issue. Introduced a fact-checking
checklist for all opinion columnists and held brief pre-publication
meetings to discuss sourcing standards.

**Result:** Reduced published corrections in the opinion section from an
average of three per month to fewer than one. Reader-satisfaction survey
scores for the section rose from 3.1 to 4.2 on a 5-point scale.

**Resume bullet:**
*Edited 6–8 opinion pieces per week as section editor of the student
newspaper for two semesters, cutting published corrections by 70 % and
raising reader-satisfaction scores from 3.1 to 4.2 / 5.*

**Formula used:** CAR

### 3. Academic Paper — Sociology Honors Course

**Context:** In a sociology honors seminar, each student was required to
produce an original research paper suitable for submission to an
undergraduate journal. The topic chosen was the relationship between
social-media use and perceived social isolation among community-college
students.

**Action:** Designed and distributed an online survey (n = 112), ran
correlation and multiple-regression analyses in SPSS, and wrote a
22-page paper following ASA style guidelines. Incorporated feedback from
two peer-review rounds and revised the discussion section to address
alternative explanations raised by the instructor.

**Result:** Received the highest grade in the seminar and submitted the
paper to the *Undergraduate Journal of Sociology*, where it was accepted
for publication in the spring issue.

**Resume bullet:**
*Authored a 22-page original research paper on social media and social
isolation (n = 112 survey respondents), earning publication in the
Undergraduate Journal of Sociology after two rounds of peer review.*

**Formula used:** Narrative

### 4. Report Writing — Nonprofit Program Evaluation

**Context:** A local youth-mentoring nonprofit needed to submit a
program-evaluation report to its primary funder but lacked staff capacity
to compile and analyze data from the past fiscal year.

**Action:** Volunteered to write the 12-page report during a semester-long
service-learning placement. Collected attendance records, mentor-matching
data, and pre/post surveys from four program sites. Synthesized
quantitative results into tables and charts and wrote narrative
summaries connecting outcomes to the organization's theory of change.

**Result:** The funder approved the nonprofit's $60,000 renewal grant
without revisions, and the executive director cited the report's clarity
as a factor in the smooth approval process.

**Resume bullet:**
*Wrote a 12-page program-evaluation report synthesizing data from 4
sites for a youth-mentoring nonprofit, directly supporting the approval
of a $60 K grant renewal without funder revisions.*

**Formula used:** CAR

### 5. Freelance Content Creation — Small-Business Clients

**Context:** After graduation, several small businesses in the local area
needed website and social-media copy but could not afford a full-time
marketing hire. Previous DIY copy on their sites was inconsistent in tone
and lacked calls to action.

**Action:** Took on three small-business clients over six months, auditing
existing website copy, developing a brand-voice guide for each client,
and writing or rewriting 25+ web pages and 40 social-media captions.
Coordinated with a freelance graphic designer to ensure visual and
verbal consistency.

**Result:** One client reported a 30 % increase in online appointment
bookings within three months of the website relaunch. All three clients
retained ongoing monthly content-creation contracts.

**Resume bullet:**
*Rewrote 25+ web pages and 40 social-media captions for 3 small-business
clients as a freelance content creator, contributing to a 30 % increase
in online bookings for one client and securing ongoing retainer
contracts with all three.*

**Formula used:** XYZ
