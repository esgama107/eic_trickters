# Program Coordination — Resume Bullet Examples

> These examples illustrate how to frame leadership, event planning, and
> coordination experience as strong resume bullets — even when the role
> was unpaid or part of a student organization.

## Bullet Formulas Used in This File

| Formula | Structure | Best For |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **XYZ** | "Accomplished **X** as measured by **Y**, by doing **Z**" | Roles where you can point to a clear metric |
| **CAR** | **Challenge → Action → Result** | Situations where you solved a visible problem |
| **Narrative** | A concise story arc in one sentence (who, what, so-what) | Experiences that are best understood through context |

---

### 1. Student Organization President — Pre-Law Society

**Context:** The campus Pre-Law Society had experienced declining
membership for two consecutive years, dropping from 45 active members to
18. The organization risked losing its student-government funding
allocation if membership did not rebound.

**Action:** Took over as president and implemented three changes:
launched a monthly "Ask a Law Student" panel series with alumni at local
law schools, created an Instagram account with weekly LSAT-prep tips, and
partnered with the career center to host a legal-careers networking
night.

**Result:** Grew active membership from 18 to 52 within one academic year,
secured a 40 % increase in student-government funding, and the
networking night drew 75 attendees including 10 practicing attorneys.

**Resume bullet:**
*Grew Pre-Law Society membership from 18 to 52 in one year by launching
alumni panels, a social-media strategy, and a networking night with 75
attendees, securing a 40 % increase in student-government funding.*

**Formula used:** XYZ

### 2. Event Planning — Cultural Heritage Festival

**Context:** The university's multicultural affairs office wanted to host
its first large-scale cultural heritage festival but had no prior event
template and a limited budget of $2,500.

**Action:** Chaired a 6-person planning committee, delegating tasks such
as vendor outreach, performer booking, and marketing. Negotiated in-kind
food donations from four local restaurants, secured a free venue through
the student-union board, and coordinated day-of logistics including
setup, sound, and volunteer check-in.

**Result:** The festival attracted 400+ attendees, stayed $200 under
budget, and received a 4.6 / 5 satisfaction rating in post-event
surveys. The office made it an annual tradition the following year.

**Resume bullet:**
*Planned and executed a 400-attendee cultural heritage festival on a
$2,500 budget by leading a 6-person committee, negotiating in-kind
donations, and securing a free venue — earning a 4.6 / 5 attendee
satisfaction rating.*

**Formula used:** Narrative

### 3. Volunteer Coordination — Habitat for Humanity Campus Chapter

**Context:** The campus Habitat for Humanity chapter struggled with
volunteer no-shows on build days, with an average attendance rate of only
55 % of registered volunteers, slowing construction timelines.

**Action:** Redesigned the volunteer sign-up process by introducing an
online scheduling tool with automated reminders sent 48 and 24 hours
before each build. Created a "buddy system" pairing first-time
volunteers with experienced builders and organized a brief orientation
session at the start of each build day.

**Result:** Volunteer show-up rate increased from 55 % to 82 % over one
semester, and the chapter completed its two-house commitment three weeks
ahead of the regional deadline.

**Resume bullet:**
*Increased volunteer attendance from 55 % to 82 % for Habitat for
Humanity build days by implementing automated reminders and a buddy
system, enabling the chapter to finish 2 houses 3 weeks ahead of
schedule.*

**Formula used:** CAR

### 4. Internship Project Management — City Government Office

**Context:** During a summer internship with a city planning department,
the office needed to digitize 10 years of paper-based public-comment
records so staff could search them when reviewing new zoning proposals.

**Action:** Scoped the project, created a timeline with weekly
milestones, and recruited two fellow interns to help. Developed a
standardized naming convention and folder structure, scanned and indexed
over 1,800 documents, and trained three full-time staff members on the
new digital filing system before the internship ended.

**Result:** Reduced average record-retrieval time from roughly 20 minutes
per search to under 2 minutes. The department continued using the system
and naming conventions after the internship cohort departed.

**Resume bullet:**
*Digitized and indexed 1,800+ public-comment records for a city planning
department, cutting retrieval time from 20 minutes to under 2 by
designing a searchable filing system and training 3 staff members on its
use.*

**Formula used:** XYZ

### 5. Community Outreach — Health-Screening Initiative

**Context:** A student-run public-health organization wanted to provide
free blood-pressure and glucose screenings at community sites, but
previous outreach efforts reached fewer than 30 residents per event
because of low awareness and inconvenient locations.

**Action:** Mapped high-foot-traffic community locations — a laundromat,
two churches, and a farmers' market — and negotiated permission to set up
screening stations. Designed bilingual flyers and partnered with a local
clinic to supply trained nursing students. Coordinated scheduling,
supplies, and data-recording procedures for four weekend events.

**Result:** Screened 145 community members across four events (up from
fewer than 30), identified 18 individuals with readings outside normal
ranges who were connected to follow-up care, and established a recurring
partnership with the clinic for future semesters.

**Resume bullet:**
*Coordinated 4 community health-screening events reaching 145 residents
(up from < 30) by mapping high-traffic sites and partnering with a local
clinic, connecting 18 individuals with abnormal readings to follow-up
care.*

**Formula used:** CAR
